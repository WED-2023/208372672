    • about me

    When I was a child, my family and I moved from Turkey to South Tel Aviv. We lived in a shared apartment with other families, which was quite crowded. My father was a tailor, and I spent a lot of my childhood working in his shop, learning the trade.

    At the age of 21, I got married and moved to Bat Yam, where my husband and I started our own family and raised two children. For many years, I worked in the diamond industry, cutting and polishing diamonds with precision and dedication.

    Now, I live happily in Ramat Gan and have four wonderful grandchildren who bring me immense joy.

    Main Differences between Web1, Web2, and Web3:

    Web1:

    Web1, was characterized by primarily static web pages. Users would mainly consume information from these pages without much interaction or participation. It was the early stage of the internet where the focus was on presenting information to users.

    Web2:

    Web2, brought about dynamic and interactive web pages. It facilitated user-generated content through social media platforms, forums, and blogs. This era marked a shift towards greater user engagement and collaboration, where users could interact with each other and contribute their content to the web.

    Web3:

    Web3, represents a decentralized and distributed web. It emphasizes privacy, security, and user control over data. This evolution integrates blockchain technology and smart contracts, offering transparent and trustless transactions while empowering users to have ownership and sovereignty over their digital assets and identities.

    Explanation of Differences with Use Case

    Web1:

    On Web1, buying a product online would typically involve visiting static web pages of e-commerce stores.
    Users would browse through product listings and make purchases by providing their information through basic forms.
    Example: Visiting a static online store, selecting a product, and filling out a form with shipping and payment details.

    Web2:

    In Web2, the online shopping experience becomes more interactive and social.
    Users can read and leave reviews, interact with other shoppers through comments or forums, and receive personalized recommendations based on their browsing history.
    Example: Shopping on an e-commerce platform where users can read product reviews from other customers, ask questions, and engage in discussions about products.

    Web3:

    With Web3, the online shopping experience can be decentralized and powered by blockchain technology.
    Users have more control over their data and transactions, with options for secure peer-to-peer payments and transparent supply chain tracking.
    Example: Using a decentralized marketplace built on blockchain where users can buy products directly from sellers, with transactions recorded on the blockchain for transparency and security.
